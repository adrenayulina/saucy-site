About

I'm Nayul — born by the ocean, raised across continents, and constantly eating my way through wherever I land. This is where I keep track of spots I'd actually send my friends to. No sponsored picks, no tourist traps, just places that made me text someone "you have to go here."

Busan 🇰🇷
Born salty by the oceanside. Raised on gukbap and raw fish before I had any idea what "fine dining" meant.
Charleston, Savannah, Austin 🇺🇸
Moved here at 15 — alone, nerdy, straight A's, surviving entirely on mac and cheese with ketchup and tabasco. Then just kept going south.
Cádiz 🇪🇸
Turned into a fiestera. Discovered that 10pm is a perfectly reasonable dinner time and that ham is a food group. De Cái picha (iykyk).
Montevideo, Buenos Aires 🇺🇾🇦🇷
Interned by day, sipping on mate, grilled asado by night. Got dangerously good at finding cheap wine.
Seoul 🇰🇷
Started my career, then started my company. Ate through every neighborhood while building something from scratch.
San Francisco 🇺🇸
Current chapter. Mostly caved up working 15-hour days, surviving on Keurig coffee and delivery. Still adding new spots — slowly resurfacing into the sunlight.

Seoul

My top picks across the city — 12 spots, 5 neighborhoods, zero reservations.

My saved list keeps growing — these are the ones I keep going back to.

01
Seongsu 성수
Eats
Somunnan Seongsu Gamjatang
소문난 성수감자탕

Legendary pork bone soup, open 24 hours. The broth is deeply savory and the meat falls right off the bone. A true Seoul institution.

Nayul's Order Come with 2+ people. Order the soup, add 수제비 (sujebi — hand-torn Korean gnocchi) that soaks up the broth, then finish with fried rice.
"볶음밥 해 주세요!"bokkeumbap hae ju se yo! — "Fried rice, please!"

03
Seochon 서촌
Eats
Tosokchon Samgyetang
토속촌 삼계탕

The most iconic samgyetang in Seoul. Whole chicken stuffed with rice, dates, and ginseng — deeply nourishing comfort food.

Also ConsiderMuguok (무구옥) is genuinely amazing, but the line is simply too crazy. Tosokchon is more manageable.

A really cute cocktail bar hidden in a Seochon alley, housed in a traditional hanok. There's no menu — just tell the bartender what flavors you like and they'll craft something for you.

04
Insadong 인사동
Craft Beer
Seoul Gypsy OG
서울집시 순라길점

Seoul's best craft beer. The original taproom — cozy alley vibes, incredible house beers, and solid anju. Try their aged red wine barrel lager if available.

Eats
Toenmarujip Doenjang Yesul
툇마루집 된장예술

Super hearty traditional Korean food — the kind of place where the regulars are all loveable Korean grandparents. That's how you know it's legit. The doenjang dishes here are art.

Nayul's OrderTry the 된장비빔밥 (doenjang bibimbap) and wash it down with 주전자 막걸리 (kettle makgeolli).
Google Maps
★ 4.1 · 446 reviews11:30am–9pm, closed Sun
Tea
Jidaebang
지대방

A beautiful traditional tea house right on Insadong-gil. The owners grow their own tea in the mountains and serve it fresh. Warm, old-school — a perfect place to slow down.

What to Try쌍화탕 (Ssanghwatang) — warm herbal energy tonic brewed from medicinal roots. Restores your body after a long day.

십전대보차 (Sipjeondaebocha) — "ten-herb vitality tea," a nourishing blend for energy and circulation.
Google Maps
★ 4.7 · 153 reviews10:30am–10:30pm daily
05
Anguk 안국
Experience
Changdeokgung Secret Garden
창덕궁 비밀의 정원
Photo: Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 4.0

One of my favourite spots in the world. The rear garden of Changdeokgung Palace — a UNESCO World Heritage site where centuries-old trees meet exquisite royal architecture.

ImportantBook the guided tour in advance! Tours run Tue–Sun and fill up fast, especially in autumn. Check the official site for time slots.

Nayul's OrderAsk about their seasonal pour-over — it changes regularly and is always exceptional.

Eats
Cheonha Bossam
천하보쌈

Melt-in-your-mouth bossam — tender boiled pork belly wrapped in fresh lettuce with incredible house-made kimchi and ssamjang. Simple, perfect, unforgettable.

Nayul's OrderOrder the 보쌈정식 (Bossam Jungsik) set — comes with all the fixings. Go at opening time on weekdays to avoid the line.
